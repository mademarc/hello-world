Tips for making your dog's coat healthy and shiny:

  • A diet rich in fatty acids
  • Frequent grooming
  • Bathing

Types of coats

Wire Coat

Wire coated dogs like the German Pointer have fur with a wire-like texture. This type of fur is stiff and tends to point away from the body, particularly on the tail and face. Maintenance of a wire coat requires removing old hair by hand and clipping existing hair to soften it's texture over time

A Guide to Dog Coats

Double Coat

Many longer-haired breeds of dog have double coats, meaning that they have an undercoat that is shorter than the outer coat. The hair is typically dense and woolly, and is more prone to shedding. Dogs with double coats require extra attention when grooming to ensure that the undercoat does not become matted

A dog's coat helps them stay warm in the winter, and cool in the summer, by providing insulation. Shaving a dog's fur in the summer is not recommended because it can slow down the process of heat absorption. As the weather warms, dogs may shed their thicker winter undercoat, and grooming can help remove dead hair and increase air circulation near the skin.

Smooth Coat

Smooth coated dogs like Great Danes have silky, short hair that lays close to the body. Smooth coated may also refer to shorter fur in breeds with multiple coat types

Grooming Tips

When bathing a dog, follow the steps below:

  • First, brush your dog
  • Wet the dog using lukewarm water
  • Shampoo the dog
  • Rinse well
  • Towel off or air dry the dog
  • Provide the dog with treats
